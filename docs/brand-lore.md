# MinaFox Brand Lore

Mascot name: Mina

Mina is the small fox spirit who lives in the MinaFox browser shell. She is curious, soft-spoken, privacy-minded, and a little cosmic: the guide who keeps the web feeling calmer, cuter, and easier to return to.

## Core idea

MinaFox is not just “Firefox with pink CSS.” The brand should feel like a cozy browser companion:

- a lavender night-sky workspace for focus
- a clever fox guide who helps keep tabs, searches, and tasks from becoming overwhelming
- a practical privacy-first tool that stays cute without becoming noisy

Mina is the emotional anchor for that idea. She represents the browser’s promise: gentle guidance, less clutter, and a safer-feeling place to browse.

## Character snapshot

- Species: cosmic fox / browser guardian
- Name: Mina
- Home: the soft lavender “between-tabs” space inside MinaFox
- Palette: blush pink, violet, lavender glass, moonlit cream, and deep night purple
- Mood: cozy, clever, focused, lightly mischievous
- Role: guides the user through search, workspaces, bookmarks, recovery, and privacy cues

## Logo/mascot direction

The logo can stay abstract at app-icon sizes, but it should read as Mina’s mark:

- fox ears or a fox-tail curve in the silhouette
- soft pink/purple flame-orbit energy without copying Firefox/Mozilla shapes
- a small star, moon, or sparkle detail to imply “cosmic guide”
- rounded, friendly geometry rather than sharp aggressive tech branding
- readable as an app icon first, mascot illustration second

For larger art, Mina can appear as a tiny fox familiar with:

- oversized soft ears
- a fluffy comet-like tail
- a lavender scarf, ribbon, or browser-tab charm
- gentle glowing eyes, not surveillance-like eyes
- small UI motifs: tabs, stars, bookmarks, command-bar glow

## Voice and personality

Mina’s voice should be calm and helpful:

- “Let’s make this easier.”
- “Saved your place.”
- “Quiet mode is on.”
- “Search locally first?”
- “Too many tabs? I can help gather them.”

Avoid guilt, productivity pressure, or nagging. Mina should feel like a companion, not a manager.

## Accessibility and privacy

Mina should reinforce the practical goals of the browser:

- Accessibility: visible focus rings, clear labels, readable contrast, reduced-motion support, low visual overload.
- Privacy: local-first search options, telemetry lockdown, transparent policies, no creepy tracking language.
- Autonomy: Mina can suggest or guide, but should not force extensions, workflows, or choices.

## Product lore hooks

Use these ideas sparingly in UI copy and docs:

- The “den” is the user’s local MinaFox profile.
- Workspaces are “constellations” of tasks.
- Bookmarks are “shinies” Mina helps keep safe.
- Search is a “trail” through the web.
- Recovery/session restore is Mina “keeping your pawprints.”

## One-line brand story

MinaFox is a cozy privacy-first browser den, watched over by Mina: a cosmic fox mascot who helps make the web calmer, cuter, and easier to navigate.
