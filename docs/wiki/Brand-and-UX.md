# Brand and UX

MinaFox is a cozy privacy-first browser den watched over by Mina, a cosmic fox mascot who makes browsing calmer, cuter, and easier to navigate.

## Brand principles

- **Calm first** — avoid noisy motion, alerts, or pressure.
- **Private by default** — local-first search and AI modes should be obvious.
- **Kawaii but practical** — cute visuals must not reduce readability.
- **Gentle focus** — visible focus states, comfortable spacing, and readable contrast.
- **No forced workflows** — Mina suggests and guides; she does not nag.

## Visual palette

- Deep plum / midnight purple backgrounds.
- Soft lavender glass surfaces.
- Pink, rose, magenta, and violet accents.
- White/cream sparkles and text highlights.

## Mascot: Mina

Mina is a small cosmic fox/browser guardian with a blush pink and violet palette, oversized soft ears, a fluffy comet-like tail, gentle eyes, and browser/tab/star/bookmark motifs.

## UI language

Good copy:

- “Let’s make this easier.”
- “Saved your place.”
- “Quiet mode is on.”
- “Search locally first?”

Avoid guilt, productivity pressure, scary warnings unless needed for safety, and vague AI hype.

## Accessibility rules

- Maintain visible `:focus-visible` rings.
- Support `prefers-reduced-motion`.
- Keep mobile touch targets comfortable.
- Avoid small low-contrast text over busy gradients.
- Test 360 px, 390 px, 430 px, and 768 px widths.

## Product lore hooks

Use sparingly:

- The “den” is the local MinaFox profile.
- Workspaces are constellations.
- Bookmarks are shinies.
- Search is a trail through the web.
- Session recovery is Mina keeping pawprints.
